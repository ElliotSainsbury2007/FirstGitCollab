Pros of LLMs

  1. Learning Aid & Tutoring
    • LLMs can explain complex concepts in simpler terms, offer examples, and adapt explanations to your understanding level.
    • They can act as on-demand tutors, helping with subjects like coding, writing, or data analysis.
  2. Research Assistance
    • Can help summarize academic papers, outline research topics, or generate literature reviews.
    • Useful for brainstorming hypotheses or research questions.
    • Can automate repetitive tasks like formatting citations or extracting key info from PDFs.
  3. Writing Support
    • Helps with structuring essays, improving grammar, and refining arguments.
    • Provides quick feedback and helps develop writing style and clarity.
  4. Productivity Boost
    • Automates routine tasks (e.g., summarising readings, generating draft outlines, or writing code snippets).
    • Frees up time for critical thinking and deeper learning.
  5. Accessibility
    • Supports students with disabilities (e.g., dyslexia, ADHD, or ESL learners) by rephrasing text or generating alternative explanations.

Cons of using LLMs

1. Risk of Academic Misconduct Using LLMs to write essays or code assignments without disclosure can violate university policies on plagiarism or AI use. Many universities now use AI-detection tools, and penalties can be severe. 2. Inaccuracy / “Hallucination” LLMs sometimes generate confident but false or misleading information. Especially risky in technical or factual subjects where accuracy matters (e.g., referencing incorrect studies). 3. Shallow Understanding Over-reliance on AI can reduce deep learning — you might get the answer without understanding the reasoning. Can harm long-term retention and critical thinking. 4. Ethical and Privacy Issues Uploading coursework or data to an LLM may breach privacy rules or university data policies. Some models store prompts, raising confidentiality concerns. 5. Unequal Access Some tools are behind paywalls or require high computing resources, creating inequality among students. 6. Bias and Lack of Context LLMs can reproduce biases from their training data or fail to understand specific academic or cultural contexts.
